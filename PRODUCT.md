# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Personal quantitative trading researchers who want to test A-share ETF timing rules locally. They are usually reviewing signals, trades, drawdown, and data freshness after updating market data or running a backtest.

## Product Purpose

This dashboard makes a 0AMV-based timing strategy inspectable: users should quickly see whether the strategy is long, defensive, or flat; which ETF was traded; how the portfolio behaved; and whether the input data is fresh enough to trust.

## Brand Personality

Calm, technical, disciplined. The interface should feel like a working research console, not a marketing page.

## Anti-references

Avoid decorative finance dashboards with glowing charts, oversized metric tiles, marketing hero sections, and color used as ornament. Avoid game-like motion or visual noise that makes risk and signal review slower.

## Design Principles

- Put signal trust first: data freshness, current regime, and recent trades must be immediately visible.
- Prefer dense clarity over decoration: use space for evidence, not spectacle.
- Make risk legible: drawdown, clear signals, and defensive states should be easy to scan.
- Keep interactions familiar: tabs, tables, refresh, and status feedback should behave like standard product UI.
- Preserve local-first confidence: when data fails, say what failed and what command recovers it.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG AA for contrast, keyboard access, and semantic structure. The dashboard should remain usable with keyboard navigation, color-blind review, and reduced motion preferences.
